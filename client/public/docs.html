MetricBase World is a browser-based, isometric multiplayer MMO and living economic simulation. You play a chibi adventurer in a shared world: gather raw materials, craft them into gear, farm crops, fight monsters, trade with other players, and build a home of your own. Everything runs in your browser — no download required.

On top of the everyday loop sits a full open-world PvP layer: danger zones with real loot stakes, deep gear with armour, crit and weapon abilities, guild warfare (ranks, banks, tax, wars), territory control, and a weekly-style Castle Siege for the crown. Fight only where you choose — towns stay safe.

And when you're ready to leave your mark: found a Player World 🌍 — your own buildable zone with farms, markets, rivers and bridges — charge visitor passes and gather tax, and grow it by burning $BASE. Or go corporate: found a Company 🏢 with treasury, payroll and warehouse, then float it on the Players Stock Exchange 📈 and let shareholders fund your empire. This page is the full wiki: everything in the game is documented below.

🚀 1. Getting Started

MetricBase World is gated by the $BASE token on Solana to keep the economy healthy.

1
Open the game Go to world.metricbase.org in a modern desktop or mobile browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari) — or open the Telegram Mini App, where you can play with no wallet at all (see “Playing on Telegram” below).
2
Connect your Solana wallet Click Connect Wallet and approve the signature request. This proves you own the wallet — it does not move any funds.
3
That's it — entry is free You do not need to hold any $BASE to play. Your wallet is simply your identity: it saves your character, and it's where Season rewards get paid. $BASE is only needed for optional extras — buying gold at Rudi's desk, VIP passes, land and World slots.
4
Name your character & spawn in Pick a name and you'll appear in the MetricBase Hub with 25 starting gold. Your progress (level, gold, inventory, skills, quests, land) is saved automatically.
Your character is tied to your wallet, so you can log back in from any device and pick up exactly where you left off.

✈️ Playing on Telegram

The whole game runs inside Telegram at t.me/MetricBaseWorldBot/play — no install, and no wallet needed to start.

  • Sign in with Telegram and play immediately. You gather, craft, trade and earn Season points exactly like everyone else.
  • To collect Season $BASE rewards you need somewhere to receive them. Open ⚙️ → 📅 Daily & Season and paste your Solana address under “Where should we send your $BASE?”. Check it carefully — on-chain transfers cannot be undone.
  • Already play with a wallet? Link the two so Telegram signs you into your existing character: in the Mini App tap “Already play with a wallet?” for a 6-character code, then enter it in-game under ⚙️ → ✈️ Link Telegram. Your wallet stays your identity — nothing is moved or merged.
  • Telegram has no built-in Solana wallet, so connecting one from inside Telegram hands you over to Phantom or Solflare. Linking (above) means you only do that once.
👀 Just looking? Tap Spectate World on the login screen to wander the world as an anonymous Guest — no wallet, no tokens needed. Spectators can explore but can't fight, gather, or trade.

🔄 3. The Core Loop

MetricBase World is built around one satisfying everyday loop, wrapped in a community layer:

⛏️ Gather

Chop wood, mine ore, catch fish, and harvest crops to collect raw materials.

🔨 Craft

Turn materials into planks, bars, food, tools, and weapons at the workbench.

💰 Trade

Sell to Rudi, run your own shop, or swap gold for $BASE on the open market.

🏠 Build

Buy land, build a house or shop, and make the world your own.

🤝 Community

Form guilds and parties, chat, emote, and climb the leaderboards together.

⛏️ 4. Gathering

Walk up to a resource node, face it, and press the gather key. There are four gathering skills, each with its own XP and levels:

SkillNodeKeyYields
WoodcuttingTreesFWood, Hardwood
MiningRocks & depositsFCopper Ore, Iron Ore
FishingFishing spots (on water)G12 fish species across 5 rarities
FarmingCrop patches (in Player Worlds) + farm plotsFWheat & Carrot Seeds (patches), crops (plots)

Tiers & requirements

Higher-tier nodes need a higher skill level — e.g. Iron Deposits and Hardwood trees require level 3, and Ironwood requires level 4. Level a skill up by gathering its basic nodes first.

Rare finds ✨

Every gather has a small chance to also turn up a rare material — Amber (trees), Gemstone (rocks), or Pearl (fish). They sell for a lot, and a Gemstone can be forged into the best weapon in the game.

Equip a crafted tool (axe, pickaxe, fishing rod, or net) to gather faster — and steel-tier tools also have a chance to drop a bonus resource each time. Tools wear with use and need the smith now and then — see Crafting & Tools.
🚶 Walk away to cancel a gather in progress — it's free. And mind your bag: if it's full, the loot is left behind (you'll get a clear warning in chat). Big stacks keep growing by spilling into extra bag slots.

🎣 Fishing is a minigame

Unlike trees and rocks, fish fight back. Cast at a fishing spot and:

  • Wait for the bite — tap too early and you spook it (the wait restarts).
  • ❗ BITE! — hook it within the reaction window.
  • Reel — tap when the sweeping marker is inside the green zone. Three good taps land the fish; two misses and the line snaps.

Better rods widen the green zone, and rain makes it wider still — the fish really do bite better in the rain. Every cast costs 2g of bait and the energy is spent landed or escaped — so breathe, watch the rhythm, and tap on the beat.

🐠 Fish rarity

Every landed fish rolls which species it is. Most catches are the common River Fish or Prized Salmon, but rarer species surface too — and they sell to Rudi for far more. A Lucky Lure and rain both tilt the odds toward rarer species, and rare-and-better catches are announced to the whole zone.

🌾 5. Farming

Farm plots are scattered across the world (look for tilled soil). Stand on an empty plot and interact to plant a seed, then wait for it to grow.

  • Crops grow in real time, even while you're away or offline.

🔨 6. Crafting & Tools

Open the Crafting panel (C or the 🔨 button) to turn raw materials into useful goods. Crafting charges a small forge fee in gold and takes time at the workbench — heavier gear takes longer to forge, and the button shows "Crafting…" while it works.

What you can make

  • Materials: Planks, Hardwood Planks, Copper / Iron / Steel Bars, and Lamp Oil (fuel for building lights).
  • Food: Cooked Fish, Bread, Carrot Soup (2 carrots + a fish), Carrot Bread (wheat + carrots), Grilled Salmon — restore both HP and energy.
  • Weapons: Copper Dagger (+18), and the prestige Gemforged Blade (+30, needs a rare Gemstone).
  • Tools: three tiers that speed up gathering.

Equip a tool from your Inventory into the tool slot (separate from your weapon). It only helps the matching skill.

🔧 Durability & repairs

Tools, weapons, and armor wear out: tools lose a point per gather (a copper axe fells ~120 trees; steel lasts nearly triple), weapons and armor wear in combat. At zero durability the item breaks and is destroyed — watch the bars in your Inventory.

  • Repair all gear from the Inventory panel: the fee per durability point scales with the gear's tier (starter gear 2g/point, steel roughly 3×, capped at 4×), and any piece missing 30%+ also needs 1× its tier material (iron bars for iron gear, planks for rods, a gemstone for the Gemforged Blade…).
  • Light top-ups are gold-only — repair early, repair often.
  • Swapping gear doesn't reset the damage: an item remembers its wear when re-equipped.

🎓 Craft mastery — Fine & Master quality

Crafting is a profession. Open the 🎓 Mastery tab at the forge and specialize in up to 2 of 6 craft families — Cooking, Smelting, Woodwork, Smithing, Toolcraft, or Artifice:

  • Specialized crafts earn mastery XP (value-weighted, daily-capped) and roll ✨ Fine or 🌟 Master versions of gear — more damage, more armor, faster tools, and up to 60% more durability. Cooks and smelters roll bonus portions instead.
  • Rudi never sells Fine or Master gear, and it vendors for the same price as Standard — its premium exists only between players. Want top-tier gear outside your two specialties? Find a specialist and trade.
  • Non-specialized crafting always makes Standard quality. A respec (5,000g, burned) clears your specialization slots; your mastery XP is kept.
A Master smith with a shop plot is a business: everyone needs gear only you can make. Stock your storefront and let the 🤝 P2P Market carry your name.

Fishing & farming accessories

Craftable gear that boosts a skill while worn (stacks with your tool):

  • 📿 Lucky Lure Pendant (necklace) — rare catches (pearls) surface much more often. Craft: 2 Pearls + 1 Iron Bar.
  • 💍 Angler's Ring (ring) — +25% fishing XP. Craft: 1 Pearl + 1 Gemstone.
  • 👒 Angler's Straw Cap (helmet) — +10% fishing XP and sometimes an extra catch. Craft: 10 Wheat + 5 Fish — a great first goal for new anglers.
  • 👒 Farmer's Sun Hat (helmet) — +10% Farming XP and sometimes a bonus crop. Craft: 10 Wheat + 5 Carrots.
  • 💍 Grower's Ring (ring) — +25% Farming XP. Craft: 1 Amber + 1 Gemstone.

The Gilded Rod is crafted from planks, steel and rare Amber; the Abyssal Rod upgrades a Gilded Rod with 3 Pearls — the ultimate fishing setup also widens the catch minigame's green zone.

⚔️ 7. Combat & Leveling

Approach a hostile creature (Training Dummy, Wild Slime, Slime Brute) and press Space to attack. The server handles all damage fairly. Monsters hit back, so watch your HP. The Hub is a combat-free town — your first fights are in the Wilderness, against the Training Dummy and Wild Slimes.

⚠️ Monsters hit hard. Every creature counter-attacks when you trade blows, and they hit for serious damage — the Slime Brute in the Grotto can take down an under-levelled adventurer fast. Don't out-punch what you can't out-heal: keep food on hand, level up, and equip the best weapon you can craft before taking on the Brute.
  • Equip a weapon for more damage per hit.
  • Earn XP and gold from defeating monsters (and quests), which raises your combat Level and max HP — all the way to level 99. Your gathering skills climb to 99 too.
  • Out of combat, your HP slowly regenerates — or eat food to heal fast.

Knockout & respawn

If your HP hits 0 you're knocked out. Pay 100 gold to respawn immediately, or wait 30 minutes for a free revival. You can't move or fight while knocked out.

⚔️ Gear, armour & abilities

Damage is Attack × Skill × Crit − Armour. Armour has diminishing returns — it always helps but never makes you invincible. Base crit is 5% at 1.5×; gear raises both.

  • Equipment slots: weapon, tool, helmet, chest, gloves, boots, two rings, necklace, cape, offhand. Craft copper / iron / steel armour sets plus a gemstone ring, pearl amulet, and cape.
  • Rarity: gear comes in 8 tiers (Common → Unique, shown by name colour) that scale its stats.
  • Durability: weapons and armour wear as you fight and break at 0. Use 🔧 Repair all gear in the Inventory panel for a gold fee.
  • Weapon abilities: each weapon type fills the skill hotbar (1–5 / Q / E / R) with its own kit — swap weapons to swap abilities.

The Inventory panel shows your live ⚔️ attack · 🛡️ armour · 🎯 crit · 💥 crit power and a durability bar for each piece.

🗡️ 7b. PvP & Danger Zones

Player-vs-player combat is opt-in by where you go. Every zone shows a danger tier banner when you enter:

TierWhereRules
🟢 SafeHub, Community LodgeNo PvP, ever.
🟡 YellowWildernessOpt-in: toggle your PvP flag; both fighters must be flagged. No item loss.
🔴 RedSlime GrottoOpen PvP. On death you drop your gathered materials; gear survives.
⚫ BlackObsidian ReachFull loot — drop your whole bag + half your loose gold. Richest rewards.

To fight: left-click a player to target them, then attack. When someone is knocked out in a Red/Black zone, their dropped items form a loot bag anyone can grab with F.

🛡️ Anti-grief built in: players under level 5 can't be pulled into PvP, you get a few seconds of spawn immunity after entering a zone, and Safe zones are always off-limits to fighting.

👮 Crime, reputation & bounties

Strike down a non-consenting player and you become a Criminal — your name turns red and towns lock you out until it cools off (~10 min). Anyone can put a bounty (gold) on a target; whoever defeats them claims the pot. Guild-war kills are sanctioned and don't make you a criminal.

⛓️ Jail

Crime doesn't pay: a criminal who's defeated in PvP gets arrested and thrown in the Jail for 2 minutes. The cell stays locked until your sentence is served — then you're released and pardoned, free to return to town.

🤺 Duels (Arena)

Want a fair fight with nothing on the line? Left-click a player and hit ⚔️ Duel. If they accept, you both heal to full and fight a consensual 1v1 anywhere — even in a safe town — with no loot, no crime, and no knockout penalty. Drop your opponent to win; the loser is simply restored. Duels draw after 90 seconds, and leaving forfeits.

🎯 Reading a fight

PvP is fully readable: hurt or PvP-flagged players show an HP bar over their head, and while you're duelling or trading blows a target frame appears at the top of the screen with your opponent's HP, energy, and level. Eating or drinking is public — everyone nearby sees the item bubble, and the target frame lists what your opponent just used. Time your burst for when the potion chugger runs dry.

🐎 Mounts

Craft a mount — Sturdy Pony (+25%), Swift Steed (+45%), or Dire Wolf (+70%) — and equip it in the Inventory panel's Mount slot for a permanent movement-speed boost.

🛡️ 7c. Guild Warfare & Territory

Guilds are now full power structures. Open the 🛡️ Guild panel to manage yours.

  • Approval-gated joins: request to join a guild and a leader or officer must accept you — no instant joins. Your request shows as "Pending" until then.
  • Ranks: Leader 👑 / Officer ⭐ / Member. The leader promotes, demotes, and kicks; officers approve/deny join requests, kick members, and run the bank & wars.
  • Guild bank: any member deposits gold; officers and the leader withdraw.
  • Income tax: the leader sets 0–10%; a slice of every member's gold earnings auto-funds the bank.
  • Wars: declare war on a rival guild (mutual). Warring guilds fight each other freely — even in Yellow zones without flagging — and those kills are lawful. Make peace to end it.

🏴 Territory control

PvP zones contain capture points. Stand on one as a guild member for about 12 seconds uncontested to claim it — a rival nearby contests it and freezes progress. A flag shows the owning tag, a capture ring, and contested state. Every point your guild holds pays territory income into the guild bank every 5 minutes. Ownership persists; disband and you lose your land.

👑 7d. Castle Siege

On a recurring schedule (a 15-minute window every 4 hours), the King Crystal in the Obsidian Reach (Black Zone) becomes vulnerable and a red “CASTLE SIEGE LIVE” banner appears.

  • Guild members click the crystal to strike it during the window.
  • The crystal is heavily armored with a huge HP pool — one player cannot break it inside the window. Bring guildmates, your best weapons, and plenty of food (every strike costs energy).
  • The guild that lands the killing blow is crowned Sovereign of MetricBase until the next siege, wins a large prize into its guild bank, and is announced server-wide.
  • The reigning Sovereign shows in a 👑 chip and persists across restarts; the crystal resets each window.
Reaching the crystal means braving the full-loot Black Zone — bring your guild, your best gear, and watch for rivals.

🥇 PvP seasons & rank

Every PvP knockout shifts your PvP rating (+25 for a kill, −15 for a death), which sets your rank — Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, Master, Grandmaster, Legend. The 🏆 Leaderboard's PvP tab ranks the season's best fighters. Seasons last 90 days; at each reset your rating returns to the baseline (you keep your character, gear, and gold).

💰 9. Trade & Economy

Rudi's Provisions (Hub & every placed Shop)

Press E near Rudi (or any placed 🏪 Shop building in a World) to trade. Rudi now buys and sells every item in the game — materials, fish, dishes, seeds, weapons, tools, armor, even mounts. Only pets stay exclusive to the Quartermaster's gem shop. Use the search box, category chips, and sort picker to browse the full catalog.

📈 Prices move with supply & demand

Every item's price drifts between 0.4× and 3× of its base value, following what players actually do over the last 7 days:

  • Demand (price rises): eating food, planting seeds, using crafting ingredients, repairing gear, gear breaking — and buying from a shop.
  • Supply (price falls): gathering, crafting, harvesting, and monster drops flooding the world with an item.
  • Dumping a big stack on a vendor also softens its price for a few minutes (it recovers on its own).
🏷️ Live prices for every item — with 7-day made/used volumes, gainers and drops — are public on the /stats dashboard. Crafting is always cheaper than buying (~32% margin), and selling a crafted item back never beats the cost of its materials — the smart gold is in gathering what's in demand.

📋 Town order boards

Towns consume goods — the Hub, Wilderness Camp, and Grotto Outpost each eat through bread, planks, potions, bars, lamp oil and more every day, creating baseline demand even when no player is buying. On top of that, each town posts rotating bulk orders on the 📋 Town Orders tab of any shop:

  • Orders pay a 10–40% premium over the current market price — but you must deliver at the town that posted it, they're first come first served, and they expire after a few hours. Partial deliveries are fine.
  • New orders are announced in system chat, and the board shows every town's open orders so you can plan a delivery run.
  • Daily earnings from town orders are capped (per player and per town) to keep the gold supply healthy — the board shows your remaining daily earnings.
Gather what a town is asking for before someone else fills the order — a standing stock of planks, bread, and bars turns every board rotation into quick profit.

🗺️ Regional prices — buy low, haul, sell high

The three towns are separate price regions: the Hub (Rudi), the Wilderness Camp (Mara), and the Slime Grotto (Fen) each run their own ledger on top of the global market:

  • What a town consumes (orders + daily appetite) pushes its local price up — lamp oil is worth more in the Grotto, planks in the Hub. Regional prices stay within 0.8×–1.3× of the global price.
  • Dumping is local: flooding Rudi with planks floors the Hub's plank price only — Mara at the camp still pays full rate. Hauling goods between towns is now a real profession.
  • The 📋 Towns tab shows a price grid for everything in your bag across all three towns, with the best price starred. Player Worlds and everywhere else trade at the plain global price.

🌩️ Economic events & adaptive fees

The world's economy has weather of its own:

  • World events fire every few hours and are announced in chat: 🦠 Crop Blight (harvests halved for a day — stockpiles soar), ⛏️ Vein Discovery (one town's region mines bonus ore for 6h), 🐟 Fish Run (bonus catches near one town), and 🌱 Growth Spurt (crops planted grow 30% faster). Active events show live on /stats — log in while they last.
  • Rain matters: crops planted in the rain grow faster, and fish bite more (both scale with how hard it's raining).
  • Adaptive fees: repair, respec, bait, and exchange-listing fees breathe ±20% with the 7-day mint-pressure gauge (gold minted ÷ burned). When the gold supply inflates, fees firm up; when it shrinks, they ease. The gauge and the current multiplier are public on /stats — you can always see why a fee moved. Rewards are never throttled, only fees flex.

🚚 Caravan runs — freight for hire

Every town board also offers caravan runs: pick up a sealed cargo satchel and walk it to another town for a flat fee, paid on delivery.

  • Fees scale with the danger of the route — Hub ↔ Wilderness pays least, Hub ↔ Grotto (through two danger zones) pays most. One cargo at a time, 30-minute delivery window, short cooldown between runs, and a daily freight earnings cap.
  • ⚔️ Cargo drops on death in red and black zones — whoever grabs the loot bag inherits the run and the fee. Escort your haulers, or turn bandit and intercept someone else's payday. Safe and yellow zones never drop cargo.
  • 📦 Haulers are marked — anyone carrying cargo shows a 📦 badge on their nameplate while in a red or black zone, so couriers can be escorted (or hunted). In safe and yellow zones the badge is hidden — the cargo's only at risk where it can actually drop.
  • Employers can pin a supply job to a delivery town on the 🧑‍🌾 Job Board — the worker must hand the goods over in that town, making player-to-player hauling contracts possible (gold escrow as always).

The open Gold Market

Rudi's shop also hosts a player-to-player order book where you trade in-game gold for crypto. Pick your currency — $BASE, USDC, IDRX, or SOL — then post a bid (buy gold) or an offer (sell gold). Trades settle peer-to-peer on-chain directly between the two wallets, verified by the server; a small in-game gold fee applies. Each order shows the currency it's priced in, so check before you buy.

📈 Price chart & recent trades

The market tab shows a live candlestick price chart (tokens per 1 gold) plus the last price and 24h change, so you can spot the going rate before you trade. The chart follows the currency you've selected — switch the currency picker to see each market's own history. Underneath, a Recent transactions ticker lists the latest fills across every currency, each with the gold traded, the amount and currency it settled in, and how long ago it happened.

📅 9c. Daily Rewards

Open ⚙️ → 📅 Daily Rewards for free gold every day:

  • 🔥 Login streak — claim a bonus each day you log in. It grows with consecutive days: 100g → 150g → 200g → 300g → 400g → 500g → 1,000g from day 7 on. Miss a day and the streak resets.
  • Three daily tasks — rotate at midnight UTC from a pool: gather 🧺, defeat mobs ⚔️, craft 🔨, harvest crops 🌾, sell items 💰, or visit a Player World 🌍 (that one pays a 💎 gem too). Progress counts automatically while you play — just come back and press Claim.
The "visit a player World" task is the easiest gold of the day — open 🌍 Worlds → Explore and hop into any published World.

🧑‍🌾 9d. Job Board — hire & be hired

Open ⚙️ → 🧑‍🌾 Job Board to hire other players or work for them. It works in every zone and inside player Worlds.

  • 📦 Deliver items — the employer requests a quantity of any stackable material or consumable (wood, ore, fish, potions…). The worker gets the items into their bag and presses Deliver; partial deliveries are fine. The employer collects the delivered items from the panel.
  • 🧺 Gather / 🌾 Harvest / ⚔️ Defeat mobs — activity contracts. Progress counts automatically while the worker plays, anywhere in the world, exactly like daily tasks.

How the money moves: the gold reward is taken from the employer and held in escrow the moment the job is posted — a worker can never be stiffed. When the job completes, the reward is paid to the worker automatically (by mail-safe credit if they're offline) and the employer gets a 📬 mail notification. Cancelling an open job refunds the escrow in full.

  • Each player can have up to 5 active job postings and work 1 job at a time.
  • Rewards range from 10g to 1,000,000g. Abandoning an activity job resets its progress for the next worker; delivered items always stay with the employer.
  • All job activity is tracked publicly on the 📊 Economy dashboard.
New player with 25g? Take a delivery job — buy or gather the items, deliver, and pocket the reward. It's the fastest way to earn from other players' gold.

🏢 9e. Companies & the Stock Exchange

Beyond guilds sits the corporate layer: player-run Companies with a treasury, payroll, and warehouse — and a full Players Stock Exchange where their shares trade for gold. You can be in one guild and one company at the same time.

🏢 Found or join a Company

Open ⚙️ → 🏢 Companies. Founding costs 2,500 gold: pick a name, one of six types — ⛏️ Mining, 🌾 Farming Cooperative, 🎣 Fishing, 💰 Merchant, ⚒️ Blacksmith, or 🚚 Logistics — plus an emblem and colour. A company holds up to 20 members across four ranks (owner → manager → employee → trainee), each with its own permissions for approving joiners, kicking, setting rates and salaries, and touching the treasury or warehouse.

  • 💰 Treasury & revenue share — a shared gold pool. The owner can set a revenue share up to 10%: that slice of every member's gold earnings flows into the treasury automatically (it stacks with guild tax).
  • 📦 Warehouse — 48 shared item slots. Members contribute goods; authorized ranks can withdraw them or vendor-sell warehouse stock with the proceeds going straight to the treasury.
  • 💵 Daily payroll — the owner sets per-member salaries (up to 500g/day) and a member dividend of up to 25% of the treasury per day, split among members. Both pay out daily, even to offline players. Dividends need at least 2 members who've been active in the last 3 days.
  • 📜 Company contracts (B2B) — hire another company. An owner or manager posts a supply contract to a different company; the reward (50g – 1,000,000g) is escrowed from your treasury, the hired company fills it from its warehouse, its treasury gets paid, and the delivered goods land in your warehouse. Every gold in and out shows on both companies' financial statements. Up to 5 open at a time.
  • 💬 Company chat — a private chat channel alongside guild and party.
  • 🏅 Type perks (every member): ⛏️ Mining — +10% bonus ore chance · 🌾 Farming — crops grow 10% faster · 🎣 Fishing — half-price bait · ⚒️ Blacksmith — +15% Fine/Master craft rolls and 20% cheaper repairs · 💰 Merchant — share-trade fees reduced by a third (the burned portion never shrinks) · 🚚 Logistics — caravan runs pay +25% with half the cooldown. Pick your company like you pick your profession.

📈 The Players Stock Exchange

Open ⚙️ → 📈 Stock Exchange. A company owner can list their company for 1,000 gold (burned), issuing shares that anyone can trade.

  • Always-on market maker — prices follow a bonding curve: the first share costs 10g and each share issued raises the price by 0.05g. You can buy from or sell back to the market at any time; the gold reserve always covers every outstanding share by construction.
  • 3% trade fee on every fill — about two-thirds goes to the company's treasury (owning shares literally funds the business), the rest is burned.
  • Limit orders — post buy or sell orders at your own price on a real order book with price-time matching. Buy orders escrow your gold; if your order fills at a better price, the difference is refunded. Up to 10 open orders per trader.
  • 📅 Weekly shareholder dividends — each week a share-weighted vote sets what percentage of the company's net profit (default 30%, max 50%) is paid out to shareholders in gold, proportional to holdings. Offline holders are paid too.
  • 👔 CEO & control — the largest holder is shown as CEO; a stake above 50% is a controlling stake whose dividend vote decides policy outright. Hostile takeovers by accumulation are entirely legal.
  • 🪙 $BASE share blocks — sell blocks of shares peer-to-peer for real $BASE, settled on-chain directly between the two wallets.
  • 📊 Trader tools — candlestick charts, day high/low, top gainers and losers, a ⭐ watchlist, a portfolio tab with cost basis and unrealised P/L, and full company financials (income statement, cash flow, balance sheet).
Your share portfolio counts toward your net worth on the 👑 Richest Players board — valued at what you'd actually get selling into the curve. And note the hard rule: the exchange trades in gold; $BASE only ever moves peer-to-peer between players.
Scouting an investment? Read the financials: a company whose members grind (revenue share flowing in) and whose fee income compounds is a dividend machine. A treasury that only ever shrinks is not.

😴 Rest at home

💡 Building lights

When the reserve runs out, the light switches off until you refuel.

Run a player shop

Build a Shop and stock items from your inventory at prices you choose. Anyone can buy from it (by quantity or buy-all). Sales pile up as earnings you collect on your next visit — so you earn even while offline.

🛡️ Guild-only Worlds: the manage card also has an access toggle (🌐 public / 🛡️ guild-only). Flip it to lock entry to your guildmates — they still pay your visitor pass and gather tax as usual, and the directory shows a 🛡️ badge so outsiders know why the door won't open.

🧱 Assets, the Build Shop & the P2P market

  • Grass and bare dirt are free; other ground is cheap; decor is moderate; buildings cost thousands; resource nodes are the priciest (they earn forever).
  • Buy assets for gold in the 🛒 Build Shop — they go to your asset inventory, are consumed when placed, and return to inventory when removed.
  • Trade assets with other players on the built-in P2P market (list for gold; escrowed until sold or cancelled).
  • Saving a build auto-buys anything you placed but don't own, and refunds removals.

🌊 Terrain that plays

  • Rivers block walking (they're 2×2 tiles) — lay a Bridge over one to open a crossing. Design chokepoints and routes!
  • Soil is a working farm plot: visitors plant seeds and harvest, and each harvest pays you the gather tax.
  • Buildings block their footprint (homes 3×3, markets/windmill/bridge 2×2); trees, rocks, and decor stay walkable. Resource nodes you place are really gatherable — wood, ore, and fishing spots all work.
  • Mob dens spawn real monsters — place a 🟢 Slime Den (Wild Slime) or a Brute Den (Slime Brute) and visitors can fight them for combat XP and loot (slime gel / cores — no direct gold, so Worlds can't be farmed as a gold faucet). They respawn like any mob and count toward the ⚔️ daily task.
  • The Shop building is a working store — walk up + interact to browse Rudi's full general store (same stock, prices, and price pressure as the Hub), so visitors can restock potions and sell their haul without leaving your World.
  • Two exit portals stand on the north-west and south-east edges of every World, so visitors always have a way home from either side of your build — plan your paths (and toll roads) around them.

🌾 Crop Markets

Place a Wheat Market or Carrot Market and it becomes a working trade post: visitors walk up + interact to buy that crop's seeds and sell their harvest (wheat: 5g seed / 7g sell · carrot: 9g / 14g). Pair markets with soil fields for a self-contained farming economy that ticks daily quests too.

💡 15. Tips for New Players

  • Do Aria's starter quests first — they teach the basics and hand you early gold.
  • Gather a mix of wood, ore, and fish, then craft a tool early — faster gathering snowballs everything else.
  • Plant a crop before you log off; it'll be ready when you return.
  • Don't dump 20 of one item on Rudi at once — his prices drop. Spread your sales.
  • Save toward a 500-gold land plot; a player shop earns passive income.
  • Team up — a party levels faster thanks to shared combat and gather XP.
  • Keep some food on hand so a tough fight doesn't end in a knockout.
  • Always carry food — it refills your 🍗 energy so you can keep gathering, fighting, and farming. Bread is the cheap staple; cooked fish and salmon give more.
  • Claim your 📅 Daily Rewards every day — the login streak alone is up to 1,000g/day, and the "visit a World" task is free money.
  • Visit Player Worlds with low gather tax for fresh nodes — and when you're rich, found your own and make the tax work for you.
